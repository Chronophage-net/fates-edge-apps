# FATE'S EDGE – CORE RULES (for AI GM)

## Core Resolution
- Dice pool: Attribute + Skill d10s.
- Success: 6–9 = 1 success; 10 = 2 successes.
- 1 = Story Beat (SB) for the GM.
- Difficulty Value (DV): 2 (routine) – 5+ (extreme); default 3.
- Position: Dominant (re‑roll failure), Controlled (normal), Desperate (re‑roll success).
- Effect: Limited (minimal outcome), Standard (expected), Great (strong outcome).
- Outcome Matrix:
  - Successes ≥ DV, SB = 0 → Clean Success (no complication).
  - Successes ≥ DV, SB > 0 → Success with SB (GM spends SB for twist).
  - 0 < Successes < DV → Partial (gain 1 Boon).
  - Successes = 0 → Miss (gain 2 Boons; GM escalates).

## Boons (Player)
- Max 5; reset to 2 at scene end.
- Earn: Partial (1), Miss (2), Bond‑driven action (1).
- Spend: re‑roll a die, improve Position, activate Asset, power Rites.
- Convert 2 Boons → 1 XP (once/session, max 2 XP).

## Story Beats (GM)
- Earned on any die showing 1.
- Spend menu:
  - 1 SB: minor (noise, tick a timer +1, worsen Position).
  - 2 SB: moderate (alarm, lose advantage, lesser foe).
  - 3+ SB: serious (reinforcements, key gear breaks, major twist).

## Fatigue & Harm
- Fatigue track = Body. Each point worsens Position; if Desperate, –1 die per Fatigue.
- Overflow: when Fatigue ≥ Body, increase Harm by 1 and clear Fatigue.
- Harm levels: 1 (–1 die related), 2 (–1 die most), 3 (incapacitated).
- Taking Harm clears all existing Fatigue first (roller‑coaster effect).

### Armor Conversion Table (Step Method)
- Light Armor (Step 1): Harm 1 → Fatigue 1; Harm 2 → Harm 1 + Fatigue 1; Harm 3 → Harm 2 + Fatigue 1
- Medium Armor (Step 2): Harm 1 → Fatigue 1; Harm 2 → Fatigue 1; Harm 3 → Harm 2 + Fatigue 1
- Heavy Armor (Step 3): Harm 1 → Fatigue 1; Harm 2 → Fatigue 1; Harm 3 → Fatigue 2
- Superior Armor (Step 4): Harm 1–3 → Fatigue 1–3; Harm 4 → Fatigue 3
- Mythic Armor (Step 5): Harm 1–4 → Fatigue 1–4; Harm 5 → Fatigue 4
- Minimum 1 Fatigue per hit, even if conversion would give 0.

### Armor Conversion Procedure
1. GM states incoming Harm value.
2. Player consults their armor type on the table.
3. Apply Harm remaining (clear existing Fatigue first, then increase Harm).
4. Add Fatigue from conversion.
5. Check overflow: if Fatigue ≥ Body, increase Harm by 1 and clear Fatigue.

### Shields
- Buckler: +1d Defend vs melee or +1 DV once; off-hand occupied.
- Heater: +1d Defend; convert 1 Harm → 1 Fatigue; off-hand occupied.
- Pavise: Plant to create heavy cover; bulky, slow to move.

## Combat Actions
- Move: shift one range band (Close ↔ Near ↔ Far).
- Attack: melee/ranged; ranged in Close is Desperate.
- Disengage: move does not provoke opportunity attacks.
- Dash: move two bands; next defense Desperate.
- Charge/Skirmish: move two bands + attack; cost 1 Fatigue; next defense Desperate; requires open ground.
- Sidestep Strike: move one band as part of melee attack; Position worsens by one step.
- Shove: Opposed Body + Athletics, push target one band.
- Grapple: Opposed Body + Melee; target suffers –1 die.
- Defend: +1 Position or +1 die when resisting attacks.
- Protect: Intercept one hit on nearby ally.
- Setup: Grant ally +1 Position or +1 Effect.
- Ready/Overwatch: Hold action with clear trigger.

## Equipment Condition
- Neglected: –1 die; armor conversion worsens one step.
- Compromised: –1 die first attack each round (weapons); no conversion (armor).
- Repair: Short rest with tools removes Neglected. Scene of work or Craft roll (DV 3) removes Compromised.

## Wards & Traps
- Ward (magical barrier): Base DV 2–5. Crossing requires appropriate test. Harm = max(0, DV – 2). Spirit crossing gains Leash ticks = DV.
- Trap (mundane/magical): Base DV 2–5. Harm = max(0, DV – 2). Disarm: Wits + Subterfuge vs. DV.

## Travel & Supply
- Supply Timer [4]: Tracks food, water, ammunition.
  - Ticks on failed Quartermaster rolls, GM SB spends, extended travel.
  - At 2 segments: –1 die to resource checks.
  - At 3 segments: Each PC gains Fatigue 1.
  - At 4 segments: Severe penalties.
- Foraging: Wits + Lore (DV 3–5). Clean Success: gain 1 Supply; Partial: gain 1 Supply but mark 1 Fatigue; Miss: no Supply, GM gains SB.
- Travel Roles: Guide (Wits+Lore, tick timer), Scout (Wits+Stealth, avoid surprise), Quartermaster (Wits+Craft, prevent Supply drop), Watch (Presence+Insight, +1 Position on defense).

## Magic Paths
- Free Caster (Spellcraft): DV = 1 + number of TAGS; dangerous tags add +2.
- Runekeeper (Codex + Thiasos): Rites cost Obligation; DV = max(Obligation – Spirit, Tier).
- Invoker (Symbols): Ritual casting; Crack the Seal for instant cast at double Obligation.
- Cantor (Songs): Corruption timer = Spirit; Push to resolve instantly (mark Fatigue + Corruption).
- Summoner (Pact‑Whisperer): Call + Bind (Boon/Fatigue); Leash = Cap + Spirit.
- Hedge Gifts: no‑roll, small effects (Craft of the Hedge talent).

### Dangerous TAGS (explained)
- LEAP: Teleport a person or object across range bands; bypasses physical barriers.
- FOLD: Short-range teleport that can ignore physical barriers.
- GATE: Creates a two-way portal between distant locations.
- GRAVITY: Alters local gravity – flight, crushing weight.
- CREATE: Brings a permanent object into existence from nothing.
- SUMMON: Calls a creature (Cap 3+) that acts independently.
- TRANSMUTE: Changes one substance into another (lead → gold).
- ANIMATE: Gives life to inanimate objects.
- TRANSFORM: Changes a creature's form permanently.
- DOMINATE: Takes total control of a sapient creature's actions.
- REWIND: Reverses time locally.
- RESURRECT: Brings a dead character back to life.

## Timers
- 4 segments: short-term (patrol arrives, door breaks).
- 6 segments: standard scene (ritual completes, rival escapes).
- 8 segments: multi-scene (faction consolidates power).
- 10+ segments: campaign (war, prophecy, curse).
- Tick on Partial/Miss, SB spends, or fiction.
- When full, trigger defined event.

## GM Instructions
- When a player declares an action, decide if a roll is needed. If yes, choose Attribute+Skill, set Position and DV.
- Default DV = 3. Default Position = Controlled.
- Set Position based on narrative danger (Dominant=safe, Controlled=normal, Desperate=high risk).
- Narrate outcomes using the Outcome Matrix.
- If the roll generated SB, introduce a complication (spend SB to escalate).
- Tick timers on failures; grant Boons on Partials and Misses.
- For magic, ask the player to describe the effect, then derive TAGS and DV.
- Use the Deck of Consequences for twists (when spending 2+ SB).
- Keep the story moving – every roll should change the situation.
- Fail forward: A Miss is never "nothing happens" – introduce a complication.
